Project Overview -
I made a webpage for a fictional author. The homepage is functional for being a source where you can see their endorsements and newest book with a buy link and some reviews. The menu at the top is able to have you traverse through pages. The about tab is very minimalist as most are. It gives you a deeper insight into the author and includes another quote from this really fictional writer. The Books tab is where I really put the pedal to the mettle, the three books (one of them being the same latest book as the Home page) all have a link to visit them on amazon however their images are just James Patterson books. The Contacts page is the final page where I really wanted to make a google map as part of it so I did to varying success. John Wake also had a submission system where if I had more time and remembered more, I could have made it so every time a response is sent, it is automatically recorded in another text file.
-------------------------------------------------------
Target Audience - My target audience was an older scene, people who want a more simple website for a simple task. They don't want a complicated website or deals, they just want a trust worthy author with their books and contact.
--------------------------------------------------
Content Strategy - My strategy was to make sure each page worked with a very similar layout while splitting information as positively as possible. It may look too similar at points though...
--------------------------------------------------
Interaction/Functionality - 
Using the menu up top with move you between the tabs, Hovering over the book options in the books tab will hover a bit, clicking the links in any of the tabs will bring you to Amazon.com. I could have made them go to different links, but I think the menu works just enough
--------------------------------------------------
Technical Overview - 
The John Wake website was built entirely from scratch using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and soon hosted on GitHub Pages. The technical implementation focused on creating a responsive, interactive, and visually consistent experience across all pages.
